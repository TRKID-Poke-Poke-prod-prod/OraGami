How Origami Was Made

Origami is an ancient Japanese art form that involves folding paper into intricate shapes and designs. Believed to have originated in the 17th century, this beautiful craft has evolved from ceremonial uses to a beloved art form practiced worldwide.

The word "origami" comes from Japanese "ori" (folding) and "kami" (paper). What started as a formal practice has become a therapeutic and creative outlet that teaches patience, precision, and the beauty of transformation.
